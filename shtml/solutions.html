Education

In our opinion, the second solution is to deal with education, which is not as passive as the first solution, but would probably take some time to come into effect as well. It would be ideal to let kids know this issue as early as possible. This topic is recommended to be included as a part of education policy.

From the Teachers' point of view, the duty for them is to avoid addressing any opinion on public affairs from victim blaming. Instead, they should teach the student not to be the perpetrator, no matter from the value of empathy or the norm of the law. Moreover, this policy should be suitable to all type of issues, for instance, if there is bullying happening in class, teacher should penalty the bully and let the class know it is zero tolerant to be violent in any situation whether you are enough with your classmate or not, and other conflict like stealing or language abuse too. No matter how, it would never reflect on the victims in violence crime. On the other hand, School is just like a mini version of society. It is very important for them to be familiar with citizenship since little. We all are part of the global world, and hold the responsibility to make it better, instead of harming it by blaming victims and giving them the second trauma.

From the parents' point of view, they could bring up the issue to establish the adequate value occasionally. Because family takes an important role in socialization, it is the first social institution for children. Parents' attitude and value on events will all affect children's physical and mental health development directly or indirectly. However, never use the event to be a lesson as blaming the victims' behavior while the tragedy is reported but having a serious blame on the perpetrator and noticing their children try not to be the delinquent in the future.

All in all, the education given by teachers or parents is to construct a victim-friendly environment, especially a gender equal world. It is okay to teach kids how to avoid dangers but not blaming the victims in front of them. They must deliver the idea of “being the victim is nothing shameful to do with” and to focus on helping them to recover or to speak out loud as you become the victim unfortunately. As the correct value are established in their childhood, when they grow up and face the tragedy like rape, they will not be the mean guy to say “It is all you deserved because you are wearing improper way” to the female victim.
